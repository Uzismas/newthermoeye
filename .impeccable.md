## Design Context

### Users
Thermoeye is a web platform for clinical Alzheimer’s risk screening using OCTA/OCT retinal imaging. Primary users are doctors, hospital staff, specialized clinics, private hospitals, healthcare partners, and future administrators/research users. Patients are mostly older adults and families who need painless, affordable, early screening, but the first product surface should prioritize provider workflows rather than a consumer wellness app.

Users need to upload or receive OCTA/OCT scans, de-identify patient data, check image quality, run AI-assisted screening, review explainable results, approve reports, monitor risk trends, and maintain auditability across hospitals or clinics.

### Brand Personality
Thermoeye should feel clinical, trustworthy, precise, calm, and forward-looking. It should communicate that the product is medical-grade decision support, not an unchecked AI diagnosis tool. The tone should be professional and accessible: confident enough for hospitals, clear enough for clinical staff, and careful about medical claims.

Core positioning: painless early Alzheimer’s screening through retinal imaging, with secure workflow, explainable AI, and doctor approval.

### Aesthetic Direction
Design as a secure clinical operations web dashboard. The interface should be bright, clean, data-rich, and controlled, with medical teal/green as the main identity color, restrained neutrals, and selective warning colors for risk states. Avoid decorative landing-page excess, consumer fitness aesthetics, generic AI neon/dark dashboards, and anything that makes the product look like an entertainment app.

The first design direction should emphasize:
- Hospital dashboard with scan volume, high-risk cases, recent uploads, and operational status.
- Case analysis view with patient/scan context, risk score, confidence, vessel density, original scan, segmentation, heatmap, and doctor review state.
- Data/insight view for risk trends, age groups, hospitals, scan type, and aggregate screening impact.
- Performance view for model metrics such as AUC, accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, F1, ROC, confusion matrix, calibration, drift, and model version.
- Admin/data governance view for ingestion, quality control, de-identification, consent, model registry, audit logs, and secure storage.

### Design Principles
1. Treat Thermoeye as clinical decision support: every result needs context, confidence, model version, threshold version, and doctor review status.
2. Make the workflow traceable from upload to report: ingestion, raw quarantine, consent check, de-identification, quality gate, AI analysis, explainable output, doctor approval, and feedback.
3. Put safety and privacy in the interface: role-based access, hospital tenancy, consent purpose, audit logs, de-identification, and secure report access should be visible operational concepts.
4. Keep the UI efficient for repeated clinical work: dense but readable layouts, clear status labels, fast scan/case review, and minimal decorative friction.
5. Use risk communication carefully: Low, Moderate, and High risk states must be visually distinct, medically cautious, and never presented as a final diagnosis.
6. Design MVP around a mock/baseline AI pipeline that looks and behaves like production, so real AI models can be integrated later without redesigning the workflow.

### Product Notes
- Product name: Thermoeye.
- Domain: Alzheimer’s early screening using OCTA/OCT retinal capillary/vessel density analysis.
- Initial target segment: specialized clinics and private hospitals because they have stronger adoption feasibility and clearer business value.
- Wider impact goal: expand screening access for elderly and remote populations, reduce invasive/costly diagnosis barriers, and support earlier intervention.
- Suggested stack from planning docs: Next.js, React, TypeScript, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, S3-compatible storage/MinIO, Redis queue, Python image processing/AI workers.
- MVP scope: login, hospital dashboard, case management, upload, de-identification, quality check, mock AI analysis, explainable results, report generation, doctor review, audit log.
- Important caution: Thermoeye should not claim to diagnose Alzheimer’s independently. It screens risk and supports clinical review.
